Dr Erik van Sebille

  • Associate Professor in Oceanography and Climate Change at the Institute for Marine and Atmospheric Research, Department of Physics, Utrecht University in The Netherlands

Main research interests

  • The pathways and time scales on which ocean currents carry water, heat, nutrients, plankton and plastic around the ocean, and the impact of climate change on these pathways.

Research

  • 2017 - present: Associate Professor at the at the Institute for Marine and Atmospheric Research at Institute for Marine and Atmospheric Research, Department of Physics, Utrecht University in The Netherlands. Working on pathways and connections between ocean basins using Lagrangian trajectories, and their impact on climate.
  • 2015 - 2017: Lecturer and faculty staff member at the at the Grantham Institute and the Department of Physics at Imperial College London.
  • 2012 - present: Associate Investigator with the ARC Centre of Excellence for Climate System Science, Australia.
  • 2015 - present: Adjunct Lecturer at the Climate Change Research Centre at the University of New South Wales, Sydney, Australia.
  • 2011 - 2015: Faculty staff member at the Climate Change Research Centre at the University of New South Wales, Sydney, Australia (first five moths as a post-doc).
  • 2009 - 2011: Postdoctoral associate at the Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Science in Miami, within the RAPID/MOCHA group, with Prof Bill Johns and Dr Lisa Beal.
  • 2005 - 2009: PhD student in the Physical Oceanography group at IMAU, supervised by Prof Peter Jan van Leeuwen and Prof Will de Ruijter. Resulted in a dissertation entitled Assessing Agulhas leakage, of which all six research chapters are published in peer-reviewed journals.
  • Published 99 peer reviewed articles, 21 of which as first author and with more than 400 different co-authors.
  • More than thirty presentations at international conferences and workshops. Eight as keynote or invited speaker; one won the best student oral presentation award.
  • More than sixty seminar and colloquium presentations at institutes in the USA, Australia and many European countries.
