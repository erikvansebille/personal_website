Erik van Sebille, Professor in Oceanography and Public Engagement

    E.vanSebille@uu.nl | Institute for Marine and Atmospheric Research, Utrecht University, Netherlands.
  • Professor in oceanography and public engagement at the Institute for Marine and Atmospheric Research (80%) and Freudenthal Institute (20%), Utrecht University in the Netherlands.
  • Recipient of a 2019 James B Macelwane Medal from the American Geophysical Union.
    Winner of the 2016 European Geosciences Union Ocean Divison Outstanding Young Scientist Award.
  • Recipient of a 2016 European Research Council Starting Grant (ERC StG) on "Tracking Of Plastic In Our Seas" (TOPIOS).
  • Board member of De Jonge Akademie of the Koninklijke Nederlandse Akademie van Wetenschappen (KNAW) and member of the Utrecht Young Academy.

Scientific interests

  • The use of (numerical) Lagrangian trajectories to track the global ocean circulation.
  • Quantifying the role of ocean currents in carrying heat, nutrients, plankton and plastic around the world.
  • The impact of ocean plastic pollution and climate change on marine ecosystems.
  • The effectivity of Public Engagement on climate and sustainability science.

OceanParcels Code

Website of the OceanParcels project, developing an open-source code to 'track stuff through the ocean'.

Marine litter tracking sites

Two websites at plasticadrift.org and plastinography.org on ocean circulation and marine litter.
